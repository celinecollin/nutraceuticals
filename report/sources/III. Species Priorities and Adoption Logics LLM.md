III. Species Priorities and Adoption Logics





The adoption of animal nutraceuticals is governed by a fundamental divergence between the high-margin "wellness" model of companion animals and the low-margin "efficiency" model of industrial livestock. The following breakdown details the species-specific priorities and adoption logics based on the sources.



III.1. Dogs



• Main Conditions: Joint health is the dominant category, accounting for 30–31% of the pet supplement market, primarily due to the high prevalence of osteoarthritis. Other key indications include gut health (25% share), skin and coat health (often for allergy relief), behavioral wellness (addressing separation anxiety), and healthy aging.

• Preferred Formats: Soft chews are the overwhelmingly preferred format (35–39% share) because they are highly palatable and resemble treats. Powders are the second fastest-growing segment, used as "toppers" for kibble to allow for dose customisation.

• Channels: Adoption is split between Direct-to-Consumer (DTC) e-commerce (Zesty Paws) and veterinary clinics, where trust in science-backed brands like Nutramax remains a benchmark.

• Growth Drivers: The primary engine is pet humanisation, where owners treat dogs as family members and invest in chronic management to extend their healthspan.





III.2. Cats



• Main Conditions: Key targets include gastrointestinal (GI) health, immune support, hairball prevention, and urinary tract support. Aging cats frequently require support for chronic kidney disease (CKD) and cognitive decline.

• Format Challenges: Cats present significant palatability challenges, making traditional pilling difficult. Producers rely on sprinkle capsules, pastes, or water-soluble powders to ensure compliance.

• Specific Drivers: Growing demand is fueled by the indoor lifestyle, which increases risks of obesity and metabolic disorders. Cats currently account for roughly 20–49% of the companion animal supplement market, a segment that is expanding rapidly.

III.3. Horses

• Main Conditions: Joint and mobility support is critical, with up to 63% of owners using supplements for performance or recovery. Equine Gastric Ulcer Syndrome (EGUS) management and behavioral "calmers" (e.g., L-theanine or magnesium) for competition stress are also major priorities.

• Preferred Formats: Supplements are typically administered as pellets or powders mixed into feed rations, or via oral syringe pastes for rapid situational dosing.

• Market Characteristics: This is a premium niche with a high spend per animal, often exceeding 90 per month. Adoption is driven by a deep performance and wellness culture aimed at protecting high-value equine assets.





III.4. Poultry

• Main Use Cases: Priorities are centered on gut integrity (preventing necrotic enteritis), improving Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR), and bolstering innate immunity.

• Additives: Common inputs include enzymes (phytase, xylanase) to unlock nutrients, probiotics for pathogen exclusion, organic acids for gut pH control, and phytogenics as antibiotic alternatives.

• Industry Structure: The sector is highly integrated, where corporate nutritionists and integrators make adoption decisions based on rigorous cost-per-tonne logic and regulatory compliance.





III.5. Swine



• Main Use Cases: The critical focus is the weaning phase to prevent Post-Weaning Diarrhoea (PWD) and manage the transition off sow's milk. Supplements serve as essential zinc oxide (ZnO) and antibiotic replacements.

• Format: Functional ingredients are delivered via feed premixes or as water-soluble additives for "rapid response" when stressed piglets stop eating but continue drinking.

• Constraints: Adoption is limited by extreme cost control and the biological requirement to mitigate early life stress while maintaining high growth velocity.





III.6. Ruminants

• Focus: Core priorities are rumen fermentation efficiency and environmental sustainability. This includes methane reduction (using inhibitors like 3-NOP), milk yield optimization, and maintaining hoof health via minerals.

• Additives: Adoption centers on yeast cultures (S. cerevisiae), pH buffers like sodium bicarbonate, and rumen-protected amino acids (methionine/lysine) delivered via slow-release boluses or microencapsulation.

III.7. Aquaculture

• Priorities: The segment emphasizes survival and resilience against viral and bacterial pathogens, as shrimp and many fish rely solely on innate immunity. Improving water quality via bioremediation is also a functional target.

• Method: Immunostimulants (e.g., -glucans) and probiotics are delivered either via coated feed or direct pond supplementation.

• Regulatory Filter: Products must be residue-free and environmentally safe to meet strict export standards and prevent the spread of antibiotic resistance genes in aquatic habitats.



In essence, adopting a nutraceutical for an animal is like choosing between fine-tuning a high-performance racing engine (the livestock FCR model) and investing in a lifelong wellness membership (the pet humanisation model). One is a cold calculation of output vs input, while the other is an emotional investment in longevity and shared quality of life.



















The adoption of animal nutraceuticals is governed by a fundamental divergence between the high-margin "wellness" model of companion animals and the low-margin "efficiency" model of industrial livestock. Across all species, the transition towards these products is heavily influenced by antibiotic reduction mandates, which have converted functional additives from optional enhancements into essential value chain components.

Companion Animals and Equine Priorities

• Dogs: As the primary engine of the "pet humanisation" trend, dogs are the dominant consumers of nutraceuticals. In 2024, 53% of U.S. dog owners reported using vitamins or supplements, up from 47% in 2023. Joint and mobility health is the largest category, accounting for 30–31% of the pet supplement market, with dogs holding a 78% share of this specific segment. Digestive health (probiotics/prebiotics) follows at 25% share. Owners overwhelmingly prefer soft chews (35–39% share) due to their palatability and "treat-like" nature.

• Cats: This segment accounts for roughly 21.8% of pet supplement revenue. Priorities include GI health, immunity, hairball remedies, and support for age-related conditions like chronic kidney disease. Feline adoption is constrained by significant palatability challenges; owners strongly prefer pastes, liquids, or sprinkle capsules over traditional pills, which are favoured by only ~15–16% of owners.

• Horses: Equine care sits in a premium niche defined by a performance and recovery culture. 63% of horse owners incorporate joint supplements into their animal's diet. Managing Equine Gastric Ulcer Syndrome (EGUS) and providing behavioral "calmers" (e.g., L-theanine) for competition stress are major priorities. Spending is high, with 15% of U.S. owners spending over $90 per month on supplements.

Livestock and Aquaculture Adoption Logics

• Poultry: The undisputed volume leader, poultry accounts for 43.5% of the global feed additives market. Adoption is driven by an integrated industry structure and a strict cost-per-tonne logic. The most used types are amino acids (synthetic methionine is critical for feathers/eggs) and gut health modulators (probiotics/enzymes). Phytase enzymes are a global standard, reducing phosphorus excretion by 27–45%.

• Swine: Focus is concentrated on the weaning phase to prevent post-weaning diarrhoea and manage early-life stress. Nutraceuticals serve as vital zinc oxide and antibiotic replacements. Lysine is the most critical synthetic additive for muscle development. Organic acids are commonly used, associated with a 3–8% improvement in FCR.

• Ruminants: Priorities are rumen fermentation efficiency and environmental sustainability. Yeast cultures (S. cerevisiae) reliably increase milk yield by 1–2 kg per cow. Emerging methane inhibitors (like 3-NOP) and seaweed extracts are targeted to reduce enteric emissions by 7% to 30%. Ionophores remain a staple, used by 90–97% of U.S. feedlots.

• Aquaculture: Adoption is driven by the acute financial impact of mortality. Because shrimp and many fish rely solely on innate immunity, immunostimulants like -glucans are critical for survival. Additionally, the segment dominates the high-value astaxanthin market, holding 46% of its global revenue for flesh pigmentation and antioxidant support.
