## Slide 1

### U.S. Pet and Livestock Trends (2018–2023)

Species Population, Ownership, and Nutraceutical Market Growth

## Slide 2

### U.S. Pet Population by Species

Dogs: ~89.7M in 2023 (flat vs 2018)
Cats: Grew from 62M (2020) to ~74M (2023)
Birds: Spiked in 2020 (~25M), ~15M by 2023
Small mammals: ~5.4M (2018) → ~11M (2023)
Reptiles: ~4.5M (2018) → ~10M (2023)
Fish: ~76M freshwater (2018), stable at ~73–75M
Source: APPA, AVMA, Pet Food Industry, Packaged Facts

## Slide 3

### Pet Ownership Rate (2018–2023)

2018: 67% of households (~85M)
2020: 70% (~90.5M) – pandemic surge
2022: ~66% (~82M households)
2023: ~71% (~94M households)
Ownership up 4 percentage points from 2018–2023
Source: APPA National Pet Owners Survey, AVMA

## Slide 4

### Pet Food and Supplement Market

Pet Food: $27B (2018) → $58.1B (2022), CAGR ~8%
Pet Supplements: $636M (2018) → $2.7B+ (2023), CAGR ~30%
Drivers: Humanization, joint health, calming, gut support
Supplements market nearly quadrupled in 5 years
Sources: Packaged Facts, APPA, Nutritional Outlook, Makers Nutrition

## Slide 5

### Livestock Population Trends

Cattle: 94.8M (2018) → 89.3M (2023), beef herd shrinking
Hogs: 75M (2018) → 75M (2023), peak ~77.5M (2020)
Broilers: 9.04B (2018) → ~9.2B (2023), flat
Layers: 390M avg. → 304M (2023 post-HPAI)
Sheep: 5.2M → 5.0M; Goats: 2.6M → 2.5M
Aquaculture: 626M lbs (2018) → 663M lbs (2022)
Sources: USDA NASS, FAO, NOAA Fisheries

## Slide 6

### CAGR and Indexed Trends Summary

Pet Supplements: CAGR ~30% (2018–2023), Index 100 → 430
Pet Food: CAGR ~8%, Index 100 → 140
Pet Ownership: Index 100 → 105
Cats: +3–4% CAGR, Index 100 → 120
Dogs: Flat, Index ~100 → ~92
Livestock: Cattle −2%, Poultry +2%, Sheep/Goats −1 to −2%
Sources: APPA, Packaged Facts, USDA, AVMA
